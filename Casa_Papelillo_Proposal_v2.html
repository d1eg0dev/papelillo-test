Why the retainer makes sense
It covers the full launch infrastructure — marketing, guest management tools, concierge services, and strategic oversight. With Luis already in place as on-site coordinator, the retainer focuses investment where it's needed most: getting Casa Papelillo visible and bookable.

It's $7,000 to launch a $250K+/year rental business. For a property at this level, that's a modest and responsible investment in doing things right from the start.

Bookings from Airbnb, Booking.com, travel agencies, and rental agents. Higher rate reflects platform fees, channel management, guest communication, and full operational handling.
Phase 2 — Direct Bookings
20%
December 2026 onward

Bookings through the property's own website or direct referrals. Lower rate rewards the investment in brand-building and reduces dependency on third-party platforms.
Why differentiate by channel?
OTA bookings carry platform commissions (3–15%), require intensive listing management, and generate higher operational overhead. Direct bookings have near-zero acquisition cost and allow full control over the guest relationship. The tiered structure creates a clear incentive to build Casa Papelillo's own brand and direct booking channel — which means more net revenue for Sophie & Oliver with every passing year.

Real Case — 7-Day Rental Operation

What a guest experience looks like from behind the scenes.

Before — Days 14 to 1
Day 14 — Booking confirmed
Guest books via Airbnb. Hospitable auto-syncs calendar, blocks dates across all channels, and triggers the welcome sequence.

Day 10 — Welcome message
Yenci sends personalized welcome with arrival details, transport options (PVR/MZT), and the curated experience menu (bonfires, boat, dining, beach picnic).

Day 7 — Preferences collected
Dietary needs, activity preferences, special occasions. Guest selects experiences from the menu — all fees confirmed and charged before arrival. Meal plan pricing confirmed.

Day 3 — Property prep
Luis inspects property: deep clean, linens, amenities, pool, A/C, Wi-Fi. Welcome package prepared. Yenci briefed on guest preferences and meal plan.

Day 1 — Final check
Walk-through complete. Welcome details (flowers, local products, personalized note) in place. Transfer confirmed. Team on standby.
During — Days 1 to 7
Day 1 — Arrival
Luis welcomes guests at property. House tour, Wi-Fi, A/C, pool, safety info. Yenci confirms first meal and reviews the pre-selected activities schedule.

Days 2–3 — Active concierge
Pre-booked experiences begin: beach bonfire, boat rental, restaurant reservation at Careyes. Yenci checks in via message — available but non-intrusive.

Day 4 — Mid-stay check
Luis visits for light maintenance check. Any issues resolved same-day. Yenci confirms remaining activities and handles any new requests.

Days 5–6 — Continued service
Remaining pre-booked experiences delivered. Meal plan running smoothly. Yenci confirms checkout details, transport, and departure time.

Day 7 — Departure
Luis oversees checkout. Luggage assistance. Transfer to airport. Guest receives thank-you message within 2 hours of departure.
After — Days 1 to 14
Day 1 — Turnover
Deep cleaning begins immediately. Luis inspects: inventory check, damage report (if any), maintenance log. Property reset to guest-ready standard.

Day 2 — Review request
Personalized follow-up message to guest thanking them for their stay. Gentle review request sent — tone is warm, not transactional.

Day 3–5 — Review management
Once review is posted, Diego responds publicly with a personalized thank-you. Any constructive feedback logged for operational improvement.

Day 7 — Reporting
Rental summary added to monthly report: revenue, guest feedback, operational notes, improvement actions. Sophie & Oliver can see it in the Owner Portal.

Day 14 — Re-engagement
Guest added to CRM. Follow-up email with incentive for direct rebooking (bypassing OTAs). Referral program invitation. Building repeat business and brand loyalty.
